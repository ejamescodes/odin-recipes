Chicken Nanban

A Delicious Chicken Cutlet with a Saucy Egg Coating

This is a Portuguese inspired dish consisting of a fried chicken cutlet coated in flour and egg

It is usually served with tartar sauce along with sidedishes such as salad and egg salad

Ingredients

  • 1 chicken breast
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1 large egg
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp mirin
  • 2 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • olive oil (for shallow frying)

Steps

  1. Make an inscision down the middle of the chicken breast so it is butterflied
  2. Make small cuts on the surface of the chicken breast to increase surface area
  3. Season generously with salt and pepper
  4. Coat the chicken breast in flour leaving no part exposed
  5. Dip the flour coated chicken breast in a beaten egg until it has adhered to all of the flour on the surface
  6. Oil and preheat a frying pan until it starts lightly smoking
  7. Cook the chicken breast until golden and it reaches an internal temperature of 165F/74C
  8. In a seperate bowl mix together the soy sauce, mirin, rice vinegar, and sugar
  9. Dip the cooked chicken cutlet the bowl with the sauce mixture

Enjoy on top of a salad with fresh egg salad and tartar sauce

I thought that this was a delicious dish. The egg coating locked in the moisture of the chicken breast. It was quick and easy to prepare, making it a mid-week meal I'll be returning to in the future.
